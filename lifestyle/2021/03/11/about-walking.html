About walking

Mar 11, 2021

When it comes to walking as exercise, most of us just assume we know it all and move on. But, do we?

This post is a little different. I wanna talk about my experience.

In my whole life so far, I always struggled and had difficult times dealing with my mental health and my mood stability. From normal daily life activities to working as a professional programmer. Over the past few years, I studied a lot hoping to find a way to help myself build the life I want. There was a lot of good content to use and learn, but to be honest, none of them helped me. Walking was one of those things. Even my therapist suggest that, but I never took it seriously.

Until I watched a video about it. The doctor explained exactly what happens in your body when you walk. And for a moment, I realized that the outcome of walking is not as simple as I always have imagined it would be. A lot of things happen in your body and brain when you walk, I don’t want to explain it here, just look it up yourself or talk to a professional about it, and be ready to get surprised!

Anyway - as someone who had the least physical activity - I started walking daily. In the first few days, it was good but nothing to motivate me to continue. But I did. I just wanted to see what happens in the long-term (more than 2 weeks). I was heard that the best benefits come after almost 15 weeks.

I installed a step counting app on my phone and kept track of my exercise. All I can say is that after almost 10 days, I started to see and feel how it has affected me. Both physically and mentally. Soon it sorta became an addiction! I learned that if I go for at least 15 minutes walk every morning, the quality of my mood and the overall day would change dramatically.

Now, it’s been almost 1 month since I started walking every morning as exercise. I know there will be some days that I don’t feel going for it, or I can’t, but the thing that I’m sure of is that I’ll never quit it.

I found out it’s my “thing”. Not only that, but I believe every person has a “thing” that can help him/her to cope with the problems they’re facing. And that “thing” can be unique for each person. All I can say is keep searching and trying until you find your own thing that maybe can help you in a long term. And don’t forget to have physical exercise. Even as little as 20 minutes per day can have a lot of good change in your life.
