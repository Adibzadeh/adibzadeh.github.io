Slower

Mar 21, 2021

I often look at time as something fast. Things happen in the blink of an eye. Different situations form in front of you and then disappear regardless of how you react to them.

But, a few days ago, for a moment, I felt it is also really slow. And it gets slower when you limit your thoughts and actions. You feel you have more space, and you can breathe. In other words, the amount of “things” you do and think about, has a direct effect on your feeling of the speed of time.

And to be honest, practically, only a handful of things are really important. Most things are just interesting. When I realized this, automatically many things eliminated from my mind. And as a result, Time doesn’t seem to be in a rush for me.

Understanding the value of time being slow helps to act on your plans more relaxed and also carefully. Instead of being fast at many things and accomplishing little, be at a normal speed on a few things and have bigger results.

I think it’s beautiful how understanding a mindset changes your perspective about nearly everything.
